History of Bishnupur

Malla Dynasty:

The Malla kings were instrumental in the development of Bishnupur as a cultural hub. They patronized various forms of art, particularly the distinctive terracotta temples that still stand today, showcasing intricate carvings and sculptures.

Architecture:

The most famous temples, like the Rasmancha and the Jorbangla Temple, exemplify the unique brick-and-terra-cotta style of architecture. These structures often depict scenes from Hindu mythology and the life of Lord Krishna.

Jor-Bangla Temple or Keshta Roy Temple

Jor-Bangla Temple or Keshta Roy Temple (c. 1655), Bishnupur, Bankura dist., West Bengal, India.

Jor Mandir Complex, Bishnupur

Built in the late 17th century, the complex features two adjoining temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Jor Mandir, meaning "two temples.

Rasmancha,Bishnupur, Bankura

Constructed in the late 16th century during the reign of the Malla kings, it serves as a temple dedicated to the worship of Lord Krishna.

Bishnpur fort gate

Bishnupur was the capital of the Malla kings, who ruled over the region from the 7th century onwards. The fortifications, including the gates, were constructed to protect the town and its inhabitants.

Shyam Ray Temple

The temple was constructed in 1643 by Malla King Raghunath Singh. The Malla rulers were fervent patrons of art, culture, and architecture, which is evident in this and other temples in Bishnupur.

Kalachand Temple

The temple was built in 1656 by Malla King Raghunath Singh, who was known for commissioning several temples in Bishnupur during his reign. The Kalachand Temple follows the Ek-Ratna style of architecture.

The gate of Rashmancha

The Rashmancha was built in 1600 AD by Malla King Bir Hambir, one of the most famous rulers of the Malla dynasty. It was designed to house the idols of Radha-Krishna from all the temples in Bishnupur during the festival of Rash.

Transport

Bishnupur is reasonably well connected via roads. Due to its importance as a tourism destination, it is connected to almost all major places of Bengal.

By Air:

Nearest airport to Bishnupur is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International airport, Kolkata. It is well connected to kolkata by road and rail network.

By Train:

Bishnupur can be reached by train and regular trains are available from Howrah railway station.

By Road:

It is well connected by road with Kolkata and surrounding towns like Asansol, Durgapur, Burdwan, Panagarh and other parts of the state.
